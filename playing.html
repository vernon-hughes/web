Term Definition
Open Strings Playing any of the four strings without placing fingers
Positions The characterization of the placement of the left hand of a violinist. When it is closest to the scroll it is in first position, and it is in a higher position when it is closer to the body of the violin
Dynamics Denote the level of volume at which a note is played, which can be controlled by how hard the bow is pressed down. From loudest to softest they are fortzando, forte, mezzoforte, mezzopiano, piano, and piannissimo. Crescendo is the term for gradually playing more loudly and diminuendo is the opposite
Vibrato The act of producing a vibrating sound through shaking of the hand while keeping a finger in the same spot (on the same note)
Pizzicato The act of plucking strings instead of playing with the bow
Legato The act of playing different notes with the same bow stroke without stopping so as to create a smooth, slurring sound
